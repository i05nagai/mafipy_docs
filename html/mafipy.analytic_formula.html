mafipy.analytic_formula.black_payers_swaption_value(init_swap_rate, option_strike, swap_annuity, option_maturity, vol)[source]¶

calculates value of payer’s swaptions under black model.

\[\begin{split}\begin{eqnarray} V_{\mathrm{payersswap}}(t) & = & A(t) \mathrm{E}_{t}^{A} \left[ (S(T) - K)^{+} \right] \\ & = & A(t)(S(t)N(d_{1}) - KN(d_{2})), \\ d_{1} & = & \frac{ \ln\left(\frac{S(t)}{K} \right) + \frac{1}{2}\sigma^{2}(T - t) }{ \sigma \sqrt{T - t} }, \\ d_{2} & = & \frac{ \ln\left(\frac{S(t)}{K} \right) - \frac{1}{2}\sigma^{2}(T - t) }{ \sigma \sqrt{T - t} } \end{eqnarray}\end{split}\]

where \(A(t)\) is swap_annuity, \(S(t)\) is init_swap_rate, \(K\) is option_strike, \(\sigma\) is vol.

Parameters:
  • init_swap_rate (float) – initial swap rate.
  • option_strike (float) – swaption strike.
  • swap_annuity (float) – annuity of referencing swap
  • option_maturity (float) – swaption maturity.
  • vol (float) – volatilty. this must be positive.
Raises:

AssertionError – if volatility is not positive.

mafipy.analytic_formula.black_scholes_call_formula(underlying, strike, rate, maturity, vol)[source]¶

calculate well known black scholes formula for call option.

\[c(S, K, r, T, \sigma) := S N(d_{1}) - K e^{-rT} N(d_{2}),\]

where \(S\) is underlying, \(K\) is strike, \(r\) is rate, \(T\) is maturity, \(\sigma\) is vol, \(N(\cdot)\) is standard normal distribution, and \(d_{1}\) and \(d_{2}\) are defined as follows:

\[\begin{eqnarray} d_{1} & = & \frac{\ln(S/K) + (r + \sigma^{2}/2)T}{\sigma \sqrt{T}}, \ d_{2} & = & \frac{\ln(S/K) + (r - \sigma^{2}/2)T} {\sigma \sqrt{T}}, \end{eqnarray}\]
Parameters:
  • underlying (float) – value of underlying.
  • strike (float) – strike of call option.
  • rate (float) – risk free rate.
  • maturity (float) – year fraction to maturity.
  • vol (float) – volatility.
Returns:

call value.

mafipy.analytic_formula.black_scholes_put_formula(underlying, strike, rate, maturity, vol)[source]¶

calculate well known black scholes formula for put option. Here value of put option is calculated by put-call parity.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{cccl} & e^{-rT}(S - K) & = & c(S, K, r, T, \sigma) - p(S, K, r, T, \sigma) \\ \iff & p(S, K, r, T, \sigma) & = & c(S, K, r, T, \sigma) - e^{-rT}(S - K) \end{array}\end{split}\]

where \(c(\cdot)\) denotes value of call option, \(p(\cdot)\) denotes value of put option, \(S\) is value of underlying at today, \(K\) is strike, \(r\) is rate, \(T\) is maturity, \(\sigma\) is vol.

mafipy.analytic_formula.calc_local_vol_model_implied_vol(underlying, strike, maturity, local_vol_func, local_vol_fhess, today=0.0)[source]¶

implied volatility function under following local volatility model:

\[dF_{t} = \sigma_{loc}(F_{t}) F dW_{t}, \quad F_{0} = f.\]

where \(F\) is underlying, \(\sigma_{loc}(\cdot)\) is local volatility funciton, \(W\) is brownian motion, \(f\) is initial value of underlying.

Implied volatility function of the model is defined as follows:

\[\sigma_{B}(K, f) = \sigma_{loc}(\frac{1}{2}(f + K)) \left( 1 + \frac{1}{24} \frac{ \sigma_{loc}^{\prime\prime}(\frac{1}{2}(f + K)) }{ \sigma_{loc}(\frac{1}{2}(f + K)) }(f - K)^{2} + \cdots \right).\]

See Hagan, P. S., Kumar, D., Lesniewski, A. S., & Woodward, D. E. (2002). Managing smile risk. Wilmott Magazine, m, 84–108. Retrieved from http://www.math.columbia.edu/~lrb/sabrAll.pdf

Parameters:

  • maturity (float) –

  • local_vol_fhess (callable) – second derivative of local vol function \(\sigma_{loc}^{\prime\prime}\)
  • today (float) –

mafipy.analytic_formula.sabr_implied_vol_hagan(underlying, strike, maturity, alpha, beta, rho, nu)[source]¶

calculate implied volatility under SABR model.

\[\begin{split}\begin{eqnarray} \sigma_{B}(K, S; T) & \approx & \frac{ \alpha }{ (SK)^{(1-\beta)/2} \left( 1 + \frac{(1 - \beta)^{2}}{24}\log^{2} \frac{S}{K} + \frac{(1 - \beta)^{4}}{1920} \log^{4}\frac{S}{K} \right) } \left( \frac{z}{x(z)} \right) \\ & & \left[ 1 + \left( \frac{(1 - \beta)^{2}}{24} \frac{\alpha^{2}}{(SK)^{1-\beta}} + \frac{1}{4} \frac{\rho\beta\nu\alpha}{(SK)^{(1-\beta)/2}} + \frac{2 - 3\rho^{2}}{24}\nu^{2} \right) T \right], \\ z & := & \frac{\nu}{\alpha} (SK)^{(1-\beta)/2} \log\left( \frac{S}{K} \right), \\ x(z) & := & \log \left( \frac{ \sqrt{1 - 2\rho z + z^{2}} + z - \rho }{ 1 - \rho } \right) \end{eqnarray}\end{split}\]

where \(S\) is underlying, \(K\) is strike, \(T\) is maturity, \(\alpha\) is alpha, \(\beta\) is beta, \(\rho\) is rho, \(\nu\) is nu.

See Hagan, P. S., Kumar, D., Lesniewski, A. S., & Woodward, D. E. (2002). Managing smile risk. Wilmott Magazine, m, 84–108. Retrieved from http://www.math.columbia.edu/~lrb/sabrAll.pdf
